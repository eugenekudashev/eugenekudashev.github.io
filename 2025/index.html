Why are landing pages so hard?

1. A good landing page is hard to write.

But it has nothing to do with writing!

You need to know very well what you want to say, and guess what ——

It’s actually hard to know what to say.

2. To know what to say, you need to do a great deal of thinking first.

You need to answer some essential questions, let’s call this “positioning”, such as:

  • who is this product for?
  • what problem does this product solve?
  • how?
  • how is this problem being solved currently?
  • how is your product different from competitors?
  • who is this product not for?

(And many others, you get the idea.)

3. You need to talk more about the person reading the page & how it’s going to solve their problems

… than about yourself, the product, the features, etc.

This thinking is hard and takes time, yes. You need to understand who these people are and what is it that speaks to them.

But without good clear answers to your readers’ questions, it’s difficult to get them to care about what you’re selling.

The point of your landing page is to convince the person to try, and eventually buy, your product.

It better do its job well, with strong arguments, not just empty aspirational claims!

Why bring someone from the outside? Why not just do it in-house?

An outside perspective is crucial because founders and teams are often too close to their product. You know your solution inside out - that’s your strength. But this deep knowledge can make it hard to:

  • Identify which parts need more explanation
  • Spot confusing jargon or assumptions
  • Address key concerns new users might have

As an outsider, I serve as your target user’s advocate, asking the questions they’d ask and ensuring your landing page speaks their language, not just yours.

AI can help with writing, but it can’t do the critical thinking and strategic positioning work. AI won’t:

  • Identify blind spots in your product understanding
  • Ask the uncomfortable but necessary questions about differentiation

AI is a tool for writing copy once you know what to say. I help you figure out what to say in the first place.
